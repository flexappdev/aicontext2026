# AI History

Key milestones in artificial intelligence — from theoretical foundations to the 2026 frontier.

---

## Era 1: Foundations (1943–1979)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1943 | McCulloch & Pitts — first mathematical model of a neuron |
| 1950 | Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" — proposes the Turing Test |
| 1956 | **Dartmouth Conference** — John McCarthy coins the term "artificial intelligence" |
| 1957 | Frank Rosenblatt invents the **Perceptron** — first trainable neural network |
| 1966 | ELIZA — first chatbot (MIT), simulates a psychotherapist |
| 1969 | Minsky & Papert's "Perceptrons" — exposes XOR limitation, triggers first AI winter |
| 1972 | PROLOG language created — logic programming for AI |
| 1979 | Stanford Cart navigates a room autonomously |

---

## Era 2: Expert Systems & First Winter (1980–1993)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1980 | XCON — first commercial expert system (Digital Equipment Corp) |
| 1982 | John Hopfield introduces **Hopfield Networks** |
| 1986 | Rumelhart, Hinton, Williams publish **backpropagation** — reignites neural networks |
| 1987 | AI hardware market collapses — **second AI winter** begins |
| 1989 | Yann LeCun demonstrates **Convolutional Neural Networks** (CNN) for digit recognition |
| 1993 | Fujitsu's SPARC workstations make expert systems cost-effective again |

---

## Era 3: Machine Learning Renaissance (1994–2011)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1997 | **Deep Blue** (IBM) defeats Garry Kasparov at chess |
| 1997 | Hochreiter & Schmidhuber introduce **LSTM** networks |
| 1998 | LeCun's LeNet-5 achieves 99%+ accuracy on MNIST |
| 2002 | Roomba ships — AI in consumer robotics |
| 2006 | Geoffrey Hinton coins "**deep learning**" — deep belief networks |
| 2009 | Fei-Fei Li launches **ImageNet** — 14M labeled images |
| 2011 | **IBM Watson** wins Jeopardy! against human champions |
| 2011 | Siri launches on iPhone 4S — mainstream voice AI |

---

## Era 4: Deep Learning Explosion (2012–2016)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2012 | **AlexNet** wins ImageNet by huge margin — GPU deep learning era begins |
| 2013 | Word2Vec (Google) — word embeddings, semantic vector spaces |
| 2014 | Ian Goodfellow invents **GANs** (Generative Adversarial Networks) |
| 2014 | Seq2Seq models — machine translation breakthrough |
| 2015 | **ResNet** — 152-layer network, superhuman image recognition |
| 2015 | OpenAI founded by Musk, Altman, Brockman et al. |
| 2016 | **AlphaGo** defeats Lee Sedol 4-1 — superhuman Go |
| 2016 | Google Brain's Neural Machine Translation goes live |

---

## Era 5: Transformers & Language Models (2017–2019)

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2017 | Google publishes "**Attention Is All You Need**" — Transformer architecture |
| 2018 | **BERT** (Google) — bidirectional transformers for NLP |

## Key Papers to Know

| Paper | Year | Impact |
|-------|------|--------|
| "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" — Turing | 1950 | Framed AI as a discipline |
| "A Logical Calculus of Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity" — McCulloch & Pitts | 1943 | First neural model |
| "Learning Representations by Back-propagating Errors" | 1986 | Made deep networks trainable |
| "ImageNet Classification with Deep CNNs" (AlexNet) | 2012 | Launched deep learning era |
| "Attention Is All You Need" (Transformer) | 2017 | Foundation of all modern LLMs |
| "Language Models are Few-Shot Learners" (GPT-3) | 2020 | Showed emergence of in-context learning |
| "Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback" (RLHF) | 2022 | Made LLMs safe and useful |
| "Constitutional AI" (Anthropic) | 2022 | AI training AI to be helpful + harmless |
